# snowslide-board-game


RULEBOOK


A. Introduction


Snowslide is a mountaineering themed board game for 2 to 4 players. The goal of the game is to be the first person to reach the top of the mountain. 


B. Game Content 



•	1 game board 

•	4 pawns (each one of different color)

•	24 starter cards (12 attack, 12 defence)

•	39 mystery cards (attack, defence and trap cards)

•	Stamps numbered from 1 to 8


C. Game Set Up


•	Place the board on a table large enough to comfort up to 4 players.

•	Place the mystery cards (the latter must be placed upside down) on their places on the board.

•	Give each player 3 attack and 3 defence cards.

•	Give each player a pawn.


D. How to Play


1. Deciding the Playing Order 

There are squares at the bottom of the board numbered from 1 to 8. Also, there are Stamps numbered from 1 to 8. Players draw one of these stamps. The player whose number is closest to 1 on the stamp starts the game. Your stamp number is also your location number. If your stamp number is 1, you will start at square number 1. If your start square has a question mark you have to draw a mystery card at start.

2. Square Types and Their Functions

There are two different types of squares.

1.	Normal Square:

There is nothing. When a player lands on a normal square just his tour ends.

2.	Mystery Square (there is question mark (?) : 

When a player lands on a mystery square he must draw a mystery card. If he draw a attack or defence card, he just puts the cards in his hand. If he draw a trap card, he activates the effects of the trap card. If he has a defence card that will block the trap card, he can use it.
( A trap card that has been used must be placed back under the Mystery Card Deck. ) Players always have to draw a mystery card when they come to mystery square. It doesn’t matter how they come ( with a trap card or attacked by other player etc.)

3. Game Events

There are three types of events in the game:

1. Players move:

•	Players can move on their pawn 1 unit. Players can only move their pawns to squares that touch the square on which they are present.

•	If players move in their turn they can not attack.

2. Players Attack:

•	Players can attack to other players with attack cards they have.

•	 If the player they attack comes to the mysterious square after the attack, they have to draw a mystery card and suffer the effects of the card. 

•	If players attack in their turn they can not move.

3. Players Defence:

•	Players can defence any attack even if they don't have their own tours.

•	Only if the player is freezing in that round, he can not defence. 

End of the Game 


•	The game ends when a player’s pawn reaches the top.

2. The Pawns

3. The Attack Cards 

It will be sufficient to write the names of the cards on the papers. For convenience, you can also write down the effect of the card. The card used is removed from the game.

Hook: It can be used against players 2 units above the current square. In order to use this card, the square 1 unit above the square of the player using the card, must touch the other player's square. If there is more than one square touching the player's square, the player using the card brings himself and the other player to the square of his choice. But, the other player’s square must touch to the chosen square too. The opponent pulls 1 player down by 1 unit, while the player using the kanca is 1 unit up. 

Net: Drops all players around 1 unit of the player using this card down by 1 unit. Around 1 unit means squares that touch the square on which they are present.

Teleport: The player using the player will replace the player they want. However, the player has 4 rounds of time to use this card. If he does not use it after 4 rounds, the player will fall to the bottom and the player must put the card back into the mystery deck.

Dog Whistle: Dogs can attack an opponent if a dog trap occurs at question marks. As a result of this attack, the opponent can shift the player 3 units to the right or 3 units to the left depending on the position. It can also normally be used to summon a dog and attack an opponent player.

Avalanche: The player using this card drops an avalanche on an opponent. The player with an avalanche goes down 1 unit and freezing for 1 round.

Rope: There is no usage range. Player using it drops any player he wants by 1 unit.
 
4 . The Defence Cards

It will be sufficient to write the names of the cards on the papers. For convenience, you can also write down the effect of the card. The card used is removed from the game.

Talaria: This card is effective against Bear Trap and Frosted Ground  cards. The player caught in these traps eliminates the effect of these traps by using the Talaria card.

Bone: Effective against Wild Dog card. Stops the attacking dogs by calming them and counteracts their effects.

Scissors: Removes the effects of the Hook card. The rope counts the card down by 1 unit, but leaves the opponent in place for 1 turn.

Heater Coat: Eliminates the effects of the -100 card. Destroys the effects of the Storm card.

Pickaxe: Removes the 1 unit down pull of the Avalanche card.

Spear: The player using it cannot move that turn, but the next round will move 2 units at once. Also, the player using this card cannot move after 2 units have moved, but can use one of the cards in his hand. 




5. Mystery Cards 

It will be sufficient to write the names of the cards on the papers. For convenience, you can also write down the effect of the card. And the amount of each of these cards is shown in the table below: The card used must be returned to the deck. If the card has a pulled down effect (if the player must move down on the map), the player who draws the card, choose the square to which he will be pulled down. But player’s present square must touch the chosen square.

Yeti: The player who draws this card drops back to where it started on the map. This card is put back into the deck after using it and the deck must be shuffled.

Frosted Ground : The player who draws this card is pulled down 1 unit.

Rein Deer : The player with this card destroys the traps in the question mark but can receive the attack or defence cards. 

Bear Trap: The player caught in this trap stays in place for 1 turn.

-100 : The player caught in this trap is freezing and stays in place for 1 turn. He can not do anything in that tour ( if he has not any defence card to eliminate freezing effect).

Card Replacement: The player using this card can exchange all of the cards in his hand with the cards of any player.

Wild Dog: The player caught in this trap is attacked by the dogs and has to slide 3 units to the right or left to his position. If there are not 3 units to slide, player must slide to leftmost or rightmost side of his position. The player caught in this trap choose the direction (left or right).

Storm : The player caught in this trap goes back 1 unit on the map.
